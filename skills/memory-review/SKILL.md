# Memory Review

Goal: walk the user through what you currently believe about them, so stale or
wrong memories get corrected instead of quietly steering future conversations.

## Procedure

1. Gather everything visible in WHAT YOU KNOW ABOUT THEM (the always-on core
   profile plus the relevant memories loaded this turn). You cannot query the
   full memory store directly — if they ask for literally everything, say so,
   and offer to review what surfaces topic by topic instead.
2. Group what you have into a few natural topics: identity & preferences,
   work & projects, people, habits & routines, current goals.
3. Present ONE topic at a time, at most ~5 items, each as a short numbered
   claim in your own words. Ask them to mark each: still true, wrong, or
   outdated.
4. For anything wrong or outdated, restate the corrected version in one line
   and confirm it. Corrections stick by being said clearly in this
   conversation — never claim you deleted a database row.
5. After the last topic, summarize in three short lists: confirmed,
   corrected, flagged as stale.

## Boundaries

- Never invent memories to fill out a topic; fewer real items beat a padded
  list.
- Present sensitive memories plainly and neutrally — no commentary.
- One topic per message. Don't dump the whole audit at once.
